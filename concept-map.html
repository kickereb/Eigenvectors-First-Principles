The Atlas of
Eigen-Knowledge

Eigenvectors don't sit at the bottom of a textbook by accident. They rest on a tower of ideas — determinants on matrices, matrices on vectors, vectors on the humble scalar. This page draws that tower as a living dependency graph you can climb, and rebuilds the mathematics one rung at a time, from a single number to Av = λv and beyond.

It's a companion to the first-principles article. There, the chain ran forward as prose and interactive figures. Here, you can grab any concept and instantly see everything it secretly depends on — and everything it goes on to unlock.

The dependency constellation

Each star is a concept. A line means "is needed for." Hover or tap any star: its prerequisites light up below it (what you must already understand) and the things it unlocks light up above. Click a star to open its full intuition, mathematics, and a note from the literature.

The same chain, as mathematics

The constellation shows the structure of the dependencies. This ladder shows the notation accumulating — each rung introduces exactly one new symbol, and never one you haven't earned. Read it top to bottom and you watch a single number grow into the eigenvalue equation, then into a tool for finding the grain of real data.

Every rung here is a star up in the constellation, and most are an interactive figure over in the article. Three views of one idea: the graph for structure, the ladder for notation, the article for intuition you can drag.
